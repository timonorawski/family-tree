## Satov Surname Origin (70% Confidence)

### Etymology
SATOV is most likely derived from **SATANOV** (Ukrainian: Sataniv, Polish: Satanow) - a toponymic surname with Slavic "-ov" suffix meaning "from/of [place]".

### About Satanov, Podolia
- Historic Jewish shtetl in western Ukraine, near Galicia border
- Magdeburg town rights since 1444
- Jews settled from 16th century
- By 1900: ~3,000 Jews (â…” of total population)
- One of the largest Jewish libraries in Podolia (754 books in 1776)
- Leading Jewish community in Podolia in 18th century
- Suffered pogroms June and December 1919
- Soviet takeover 1921

### Spelling Variations
- Satov, Satow, Satoff (shortened)
- Satovsky, Satowski (full adjectival)
- Satanow, Satanover, Satanowski (full place-name)

## Fine Surname Origin (75% Confidence)

### Etymology
FINE is almost certainly anglicized from Ashkenazi **FEIN** (Yiddish: fayn) meaning "fine, elegant, refined" - a complimentary descriptive surname.

### Related Surnames
- Fein, Fain, Fajn, Feiner, Feinman, Fineman, Feynman
- Compound: Feinberg, Feinstein, Feinblatt, Feinfeld, Feintuch

### Distribution
71% of Fine bearers in North America; predominantly Jewish

## Given Name Analysis

### Irving Fine
- Not Hebrew/Yiddish; Scottish surname origin
- Was #1 most common name among Jewish American men born ~1917
- Used as Canadianized form of: Israel (Yisroel), Isaac (Yitzchak), Isaiah (Yeshayahu)

### Shirley Fine
- English name; peaked 1935 (Shirley Temple)
- Very popular among Jewish families in 1930s as assimilation name
- Her Hebrew/Yiddish name unknown
